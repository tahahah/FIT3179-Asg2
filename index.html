Top Job Categories in Australia

The biggest job category in Australia is Information and Communication Technology with 311 job postings. The pie chart shows the diversity of different job categories within Australia. There are both jobs that require higher education alongside jobs that require minimal or none higher education within the top job categories. To increase the chances of landing a job, an individual should choose the domains of IT, Accounting or Trades and Services.

Employment Contract Distribution

Most job positions posted require the hired applicant to pursue a full-time employment contract. This is the standard practice with over 60% of the job postings being for full-time jobs. To increase the chances of success in job pursuit, one must aim for full-time jobs. Casual and Part time jobs are posted significantly lesser times. This indicates that employers are looking for employees that are dedicated and willing to commit to a full time working life style.

Comparison of Salaries for Both Genders

From a cursory glance, there are discernible gaps in salaries between genders in many positions. The brown bars tend to be taller in most cases, suggesting that males, on average, receive higher salaries than their female counterparts in those positions. However, there are instances where the female average salary (purple bars) is competitive or even surpasses the male average.


Upon taking a wholistic view of the chart, it is apparent that the distribution for the female salary is consistently distributed similar to the male salary. While the male bars do stretch longer than the female bars, the female bars are not far off.
